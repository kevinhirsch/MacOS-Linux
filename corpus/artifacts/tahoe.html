macOS 26 Tahoe

In September 2025, Apple released macOS 26 Tahoe, advancing the version number to match its new convention of aligning releases with the next calendar year.

Tahoe introduced Liquid Glass to the Mac. Despite sharing a name, the user interface was different in some notable ways to its iOS and iPadOS cousins. Beyond the new design, Tahoe brought with it an overhauled Spotlight that doubled as an automation tool thanks to Shortcuts, the ability to customize folders with colors and emoji, Live Activity support, Control Center customization, and more.

Shipping five years after the start of the Apple silicon transition, Tahoe was the last version of macOS to support Intel Macs.
