Material Lab · package

Tahoe Liquid Glass for Ubuntu

The endgame — a distributable package that reproduces macOS Tahoe + Liquid Glass on Ubuntu, built against the locked baseline (calibration sheet for the numbers, rulebook for the laws). Aesthetic parity; apps you install inherit it. Not a pile of tweaks — a repo with an installer.

Substrate — settled by evidence

KDE Plasma 6 + a custom KWin OpenGL effect. Pixel-parity Liquid Glass is compositor-level, and the deciding fact came from a real project: a GNOME extension has to clone the wallpaper because Mutter won't hand over the backdrop, and it won't even load on your GNOME 46. KWin gives an effect the real backdrop framebuffer directly — cleaner, and it reaches behind actual windows. The GLSL optics are proven and MIT-licensed (port, don't reinvent). Plasma also means Qt apps inherit Kvantum natively and GTK apps via the bridge — your "apps extend the theme" requirement, for free.

02

The four layers

Each reads from the tokens and obeys the rulebook.

SUBSTRATE
Plasma 6 alongside GNOME (session-switchable at login, reversible) — or a clean Kubuntu if you commit. Pulls Kvantum + the Better-Blur KWin effect as the glass base.
apt: kde-plasma-desktop · qt6-style-kvantum · kwin-effects-forceblur
GLASS EFFECT
Custom KWin OpenGL effect. Port the proven optics — Snell refraction, superellipse height y=(1−(1−x)⁴)^¼, Fresnel + specular + chromatic, adaptive-ink flip @ Y≈0.36. Apply per the rulebook: one plane, functional surfaces only, never glass-on-glass, Regular default / Clear + 35% dim, materialize not fade.
tokens: glass.* · vibrancy.blend · material.variants · shape.types
THEME
Kvantum tuned to tokens (radii, control 28/24/28pt, tint the background not the text) + Aurorae decoration (left traffic lights 14/24/26pt) + color-schemes for light / dark / increased-contrast. Concentric radii: window 16/20/26 → card 9 → control.
tokens: window.radius.* · card.cornerRadius · accent.palette · selection.fill · focusRing
CONFIGS
Global menu (App·File·Edit·… order) · dock (floating panel, icons-only, 48/128px) · SF Pro + the type ramp · the real Tahoe-Beach wallpaper + sun-based auto light/dark (port the Phoenix timer) · springs per-app for GTK4 via AdwSpringParams · a11y overrides.
tokens: type.* · dock.* · appearance.auto · spring.* · menuBar order

05

The honest ceiling

~95% of the desktop is reachable. The residual is narrow and named: dynamic ink on third-party app-drawn text (the app owns those glyphs — even macOS only auto-applies it to system chrome), true spring physics inside non-native apps (GTK4 apps you build get it; arbitrary apps don't), and pixel-exact refraction (convincing, not bit-identical — no Linux compositor mirrors Apple's exact Metal pipeline). Everything Apple renders in its own compositor, we render in KWin.

Two ways in. The clean route is a fresh Kubuntu (or Plasma-first) install — no coexistence mess. The no-repave route is Plasma added beside your GNOME, chosen at the login screen and removable with apt — you trial it without giving up GNOME. Either way, Phase 0 is low-risk and reversible, and it's where we'd start. Every value here traces to the calibration sheet; every behaviour to the rulebook.
